7-Year-Old Horse Rider Honored for Bravery After Fall

Seven-year-old Bunny-Mae was recognized for her remarkable bravery and resilience after fracturing her femur in a riding accident, spending six weeks in a wheelchair, and returning to pursue her dream of becoming a barrel racer.

Olympian Karl Cook Says Riding a Racehorse at Santa Anita Was Wild

Olympic medalist Karl Cook prepared for the Longines FEI Jumping World Cup LA by galloping a 3-year-old Thoroughbred at Santa Anita—an experience he called both thrilling and meaningful as he embraced the challenge, the adrenaline, and the social-media fun that came with it.

FTC Grants Stay of Trainer’s Two-Year HIWU Suspension Over Rooster Meds

An FTC judge granted a stay of trainer Eusabio Juarez-Ruffino’s two-year HIWU suspension after ruling that excluded evidence from his auto mechanic—who claimed responsibility for banned syringes found in the trainer’s vehicle—must be heard in a new evidentiary proceeding.

Stetson Wright Scores Two Round Wins on Opening Night of Wrangler NFR

Stetson Wright dominated opening night of the 2025 Wrangler NFR with wins in both saddle bronc and bull riding as several other top contenders—Cooper Cooke, Tyler Waguespack, Lightning Aguilera/Kaden Profili, Shad Mayfield, and Kassie Mowry—also kicked off the rodeo with standout Round 1 victories.

Exceedingly Rare 1919 Kentucky Derby Ticket Sells on eBay

A remarkably scarce general admission ticket from the 1919 Kentucky Derby—Sir Barton’s Triple Crown–clinching year and one of only two such tickets known to experts—sold on eBay for $35,000, coming within $10,000 of the all-time record for Derby ticket prices.

Canadian Hall of Fame Trainer Mike Doyle Dies

Canadian Hall of Fame trainer Mike Doyle, celebrated for a storied career that included 1,178 wins, 29 stakes victories, and over $44 million in earnings, has passed away at 74, leaving a lasting legacy on Canadian and international horse racing.
