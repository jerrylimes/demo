Massachusetts Rider Seriously Injured and Horse Killed After Trail Encounter with Cyclist

A Massachusetts rider recovering from severe injuries is speaking out about trail safety after her horse fatally bolted when a fast-approaching cyclist reportedly spooked him during a ride, leading to a tragic fall and the horse’s euthanasia.

Meet the NFR Barrel Racing Horses Set to Shine in Round 1

After a tense period marked by an EHV-1 outbreak and uncertainty surrounding the NFR, barrel racers have finalized their Round 1 horse selections, revealing a deep and diverse lineup of veteran champions, rising stars, and resilient teams heading into the Thomas & Mack Arena.

James McDonald Secures Third Longines World’s Best Jockey Crown

James McDonald captured his third Longines World’s Best Jockey title with a commanding 184-point season fueled by 12 Group/Grade 1 wins—led by standout performances aboard Romantic Warrior, Voyage Bubble, Via Sistina, and other top runners—that left him well clear of Mickael Barzalona and William Buick in the final standings.

Racing Cheers as Reeves Protects Industry in ‘Axe the Tax’ Budget Win

The British Horseracing Authority and industry leaders celebrated the government’s decision to maintain horse racing betting duty at 15%, calling it a victory for the “Axe The Racing Tax” campaign and a boost for the sport’s growth while other online gambling duties rise.

Mobile Implements Stricter Horse and Rider Regulations for Mardi Gras

Mobile has updated its Mardi Gras horse regulations, easing some prior requirements while adding new safety measures—including on-site health checks, revised rider evaluations, helmet rules for riders under 16, and weight limits—to better protect both horses and participants.

Whitney Family Gifts Historic Trophies to National Museum

The National Museum of Racing and Hall of Fame has received the Whitney family’s historic 400-plus trophy collection—featuring multiple Triple Crown race trophies and more than a century of Thoroughbred racing legacy—bequeathed by the late former chairman John Hendrickson.
